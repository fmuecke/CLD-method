# HYP-2026-003: Polling at ≤2h latency is sufficient for WIP control

Status: Open
Initiative: INIT-2026-002

## Belief

We believe that a polling-based sync with a maximum latency of 2 hours is sufficient for the value stream team to make correct WIP control decisions — no Epic state change will go undetected long enough to cause a bad decision or a missed WIP violation.

## Why We Think This

WIP decisions are typically made in daily standups or planning sessions, not in real time. A 2-hour lag means the board is current by the start of any working session. This is a reasoned assumption based on how the team works, not a measured threshold.

## What Changes If We're Right

A simple polling architecture (no webhooks, no event bus) is sufficient for Phase 1. Lower operational complexity, easier to debug, no dependency on BusinessMap or ADO webhook reliability.

## Falsification Signal

A WIP violation goes undetected for more than one working day because the sync lag caused the board to show an incorrect Epic state at the time a WIP decision was made. Or: the team reports that they cannot trust the board because it is too often out of date.

## Why a Positive Result Is Not Trivial

The team might simply not notice the lag during a pilot if the volume of Epic state changes is low. Absence of complaints about staleness is not confirmation that 2h is sufficient — it may just mean that the lag happened not to matter during the pilot window.

## Alternative Hypothesis

WIP decisions are made ad hoc throughout the day, not just in scheduled sessions. A 2-hour lag is long enough to allow a team to start work on an Epic that should be blocked, without the board reflecting it in time to intervene.

## Smallest Possible Test

- **Method:** Run the sync at 2h intervals for two weeks. Ask the team weekly: did you ever look at the board and act on information that turned out to be stale? Log any instances where a sync lag caused or nearly caused a bad decision.
- **Confirms if:** Zero reported instances of decisions made on stale data over two weeks.
- **Rejects if:** One or more instances where the lag caused a WIP decision to be made on incorrect board state.

## Time to Evidence

Two weeks of pilot operation — cannot be shortcut without simulating team behavior, which is less reliable than real usage.

## Confidence Before Testing

Medium — daily standups suggest 2h is fine, but we haven't observed how often ad-hoc WIP decisions are made outside scheduled sessions.

## Validation Plan

1. Run Phase 1 sync at 2h polling interval.
2. Collect structured feedback at end of each week: any stale-data incidents?
3. If no incidents after two weeks: confirm and close. If incidents occur: shorten polling interval or evaluate webhooks.
