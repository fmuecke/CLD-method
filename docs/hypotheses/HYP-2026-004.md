# HYP-2026-004: The manual Epic linking workflow is reliable enough in practice

Status: Open
Initiative: INIT-2026-002

## Belief

We believe that the manual step of creating a paired ADO Epic and writing its ID back into BusinessMap's ExternalId field will be performed consistently enough that unlinked Epics do not silently drop from the sync — and that the team will notice and correct any missing links before they cause misalignment.

## Why We Think This

The linking step happens at Epic creation time, which is a deliberate, infrequent act (~20 Epics/week at peak). It is not a background task. We assume that whoever creates the BM Epic will follow the workflow, and that the sync reporting missing links provides enough visibility. This is an assumption about human behavior, not a technical guarantee.

## Falsification Signal

An Epic exists in BusinessMap without an ExternalId, its ADO counterpart exists and has active child work, and no one notices the missing link until the misalignment is discovered through other means (e.g., a standup, a planning session, a manual board check).

## Why a Positive Result Is Not Trivial

During a pilot, people are paying closer attention than they would in steady-state operation. Clean linking during the pilot does not mean the workflow holds when it becomes routine and the novelty wears off. Confirmation requires evidence from steady-state operation, not just the first weeks.

## Alternative Hypothesis

The manual linking step is friction enough that it gets skipped under time pressure. Epics are created in BM, the ADO pairing is deferred and forgotten, and the sync silently covers only a subset of Epics — creating a false sense of completeness on the board.

## Smallest Possible Test

- **Method:** After two weeks of operation, query BusinessMap for all Epics. Count how many have no ExternalId set. Cross-check: do any of those have active ADO child work? Report the count to the team.
- **Confirms if:** Zero Epics with missing ExternalId that have active ADO work underneath them.
- **Rejects if:** One or more Epics have active ADO child work but no ExternalId — silent gap in the sync.

## Time to Evidence

Two weeks — same pilot window as HYP-2026-002. One API query at the end of each week.

## Confidence Before Testing

Low-Medium — human-in-the-loop steps at creation time are a known failure mode. The workflow is simple but not enforced technically.

## Validation Plan

1. Add a weekly automated check: query BM for Epics with no ExternalId, log as a warning.
2. At end of pilot: review the log. Any unlinked Epics with active ADO work → rejected.
3. If rejected: evaluate options — enforce linking at creation time via BM automation, or make the sync report unlinked Epics prominently so they cannot be ignored.
